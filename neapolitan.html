Pizza dough · 00 flour · high heat

Neapolitan
pizza dough

A simple 4-ingredient dough for soft, airy 10-inch pizzas with a thin centre and puffed cornicione. Built around a long room-temperature proof and a very hot pizza oven.

NY Style Neapolitan
Active time ~25 min
Total time 24 hrs
Yield Four 10" pies
Oven temp 420-480°C

Method overview

1
Mix the dough ~5 min

Add room-temperature water to a large bowl, then mix in the 00 flour, salt, and tiny amount of yeast until there are no dry patches and the dough forms a shaggy mass.

No oil or sugar in the dough. Neapolitan dough gets its texture from flour, water, salt, yeast, and time.
2
Rest, then knead 1 hr + 5 min

Cover the dough and let it rest for about an hour, then knead for around 5 minutes until smoother and more elastic. Return it to the bowl and cover tightly.

3
Bulk proof ~20 hrs

Let the dough prove as one mass at room temperature. The long fermentation develops flavour and gives the crust a light, open texture.

Cool room Use more yeast or give it extra time.
Warm room Use less yeast and watch the dough closely.
4
Divide and ball 4-6 hrs before bake

Divide into equal dough balls, shape each one taut, then cover for the final proof. For the base recipe this gives four balls of about 250g each.

5
Shape and bake 60-90 sec

Push air from the centre toward the rim, leaving the edge untouched. Stretch to about 10 inches, top lightly, launch immediately, and rotate often in a 420-480°C pizza oven.

For a domestic oven, use a preheated stone or steel at the hottest setting and expect about 6-8 minutes instead.

Pizza oven notes

🌡
Target 420-480°C Neapolitan needs intense heat. Aim for a stone floor hot enough to bake in 60-90 seconds.
🔄
Turn every 20-30 sec Keep the rim moving near the flame so the crust blisters without burning one side.
🍅
Keep toppings light About two spoons of tomato sauce and a small handful of mozzarella per pizza.
🧀
Dry fresh mozzarella Tear or slice it, then blot 30 minutes before baking to avoid a wet centre.
⚡
Launch immediately Shape and cook one pizza at a time. Topped dough sticks quickly.

Proof by room temp

18-20°C
Use the higher end of the yeast range. Dough may need extra time.
21-23°C
Use the middle of the yeast range for the 20h bulk + 4-6h final proof.
24-26°C
Use the lower end of the yeast range and watch for overproofing.
27°C+
Reduce yeast further, shorten proofing, or move the dough somewhere cooler.

This is practical guidance rather than a precise yeast calculator. Yeast strength, flour, and actual dough temperature all change the proofing speed.
