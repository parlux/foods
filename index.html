Pizza dough · Pizza oven adapted

New York style
pizza dough

High-hydration, cold-fermented dough with a crispy, foldable crumb. Adapted from Joshua Weissman's recipe for a pizza oven at 280–320°C.

Active time ~20 min
Total time 2 days
Yield Two 15" pies
Oven temp 280–320°C

Method overview

1
Mix the dough ~5 min

Combine dry ingredients in a large bowl. Dissolve yeast in warm water, stir in olive oil. Pour wet into dry and mix until shaggy, then knead until smooth — about 3–5 minutes. Shape into a ball.

The dough should feel tacky but not sticky. If it tears instead of stretching, knead another minute.
2
Cold ferment 12–24 hrs

Lightly grease a large bowl, add dough, and cover tightly with plastic wrap. Refrigerate overnight or up to 24 hours. The slow, cold fermentation is what builds flavour and improves gluten structure.

Don't skip this. 24 hours gives noticeably better flavour than 12.
3
Divide and ball 3.5 hrs proof or back in fridge

Flatten each piece to degas, fold edges into the centre, pinch the seam, and flip seam-side down. Cup dough with both hands and rotate in small circles on the bench to build surface tension until you have a taut ball.

For 15–19" pies 2 pieces ~760g each
Proof 3.5 hrs at room temp
For 14" pies 4 pieces ~400g each
Or cold ferment again up to +24 hrs
4
Rest at room temperature ~1.5 hrs

If the dough was fridge-cold after balling, let it come to room temperature before stretching. Cold dough is tight and will tear — it needs to relax.

5
Stretch ~5 min

Flour both sides of the dough ball. Press your fist into the centre and work outward, leaving a ⅓" border. Drape over your fists and let gravity stretch it, rotating around the edge until 15". Final tug on the bench to even out.

Pizza oven peel tip Dust the peel generously with semolina — not plain flour. Semolina acts like ball bearings and won't go gummy. Build the pizza directly on the peel, do a shake test before topping, then top and launch immediately.

Pizza oven notes

🌡
Target 280–320°C Not max temp. NY-style needs 7–8 minutes, not 90 seconds like Neapolitan.
🪨
Straight on the stone No tray. Direct conductive heat is what crisps the base. A tray steams it.
🔄
Rotate every 2 min Pizza ovens have a hot side near the flame. Rotate to even out colour.
🌽
Semolina on the peel Acts like ball bearings. Plain flour goes gummy. Shake test before topping.
✅
Skip the broil step The dome provides natural top heat. Just watch the cheese colour.
